Blood

Cocoa, when consumed as a powder or as dark chocolate, can help reduce blood pressure level. The flavonals in cocoa are known to improve nitric oxide levels in the blood, which enhances the function of blood vessels and reduces blood pressure. These flavonals also improves blood supply content to the brain. The polyphenols found in cocoa, which increases blood flow and brain function, reduce the risk of gaining neurodegenerative disease. Cocoa powder also contain polyphenols, an antioxidant that improves cholesterol and blood sugar and therefore reduces risk of heart disease.

Cancer

The flavonals in cocoa have cancer-protective properties and low toxicity. These flavonals have antioxidant effects that induce cancer cell death and prevent the spread of cancer cells. Studies have shown that using a cocoa-rich diet reduce leukemia and breast, pancreatic, prostate, liver, and colon cancer. Cocoa can help when undergoing radiation therapy once diagnosed with cancer. The selenium in cocoa powder limits the negative side effects of radiotherapy.

Cocoa has compounds that have antibacterial, immune-stimulating properties. Cocoa powder also has iron, zinc, and selenium which help the body function and the immune system a boost.
